# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

Coyote3 contributors and maintainers are committed to a respectful, professional, and inclusive collaboration environment.

All participants are expected to communicate constructively, review responsibly, and prioritize patient-safety-oriented engineering quality.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful in all project interactions.
- Give technical feedback that is specific, factual, and actionable.
- Assume good intent and ask clarifying questions before escalating disagreement.
- Keep discussions focused on code, behavior, and documented requirements.
- Accept and act on maintainers' decisions about scope, timing, and standards.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, intimidation, discrimination, or personal attacks.
- Demeaning, hostile, or inflammatory language.
- Repeated unconstructive disruption of technical discussions.
- Sharing confidential, sensitive, or private information without authorization.

## Enforcement responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for enforcing this code of conduct and may take any action they consider appropriate for unacceptable behavior.

## Enforcement actions

Depending on severity, maintainers may:

1. Request behavior correction.
2. Limit participation in project discussions or reviews.
3. Remove access to project collaboration channels.
4. Escalate through organizational channels when required.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, report it to the project maintainers through official team channels.

Reports should include:

- what happened
- when it happened
- where it happened
- any supporting context

All reports will be reviewed and handled as confidentially as practical.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project collaboration spaces, including issues, pull requests, reviews, and team communication channels related to Coyote3.
